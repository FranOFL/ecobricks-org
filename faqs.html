General Questions

What is plastic?

Plastic. That convenient, colorful, wonderful and ever so troublesome material that we all use every day is very easy to take for granted. Fact is it's crazy unique... and valuable-- you literally can't find anything like it in elsewhere in our solar system. Plastic is a petroleum by-product. That means it is made from the carbon of ancient organisms-- algae, forest, dinausaurs that lived and died hundreds of millions of years ago. As they were buried, the Earth compacted and secured. Today we call these deposits 'fossil fuels'. But, it was never meant to be fuel-- making fuels leads to leftover chemicals. Turns out that though they are perfect for making... plastic! Today the vast majority of plastics are made from fossil fuels leftovers and natural gas.

Learn more: The Long Story of Plastic

What is an ecobrick?

An ecobrick is a plastic bottle packed with used plastic to a set density to create a reusable building block. Ecobricking is a low-tech, manual, zero-carbon method take responsibility for one's plastic and energize our transition from it. Ecobricks follow the Earth's example of compacting and securing carbon indefinitely. The goal of ecobricking is platic sequestration: the removal of plastic from the biosphere and from carbon-intense industrial processing. Ecobricks are then used as short and term building blocks. Furniture, gardens and green spaces can be made with them.

Learn more: Ecobrick Basics

Why make ecobricks?

Plastic is part of our age. No matter your culture or continent, you're going to use plastic! Unlike other plastic management methods, ecobricking allows us to be 100% sure our plastic doesn't get into the biosphere. Because it's hard work, it also motivates us to consume less-- and it gets us thinking abou consumption and the whole system behind it. Because its easy and because of its regenerative principles, it's a straightforward to make a meaningful ecological contribution today. And because it's all manual and collaboration powered, ecobricking brings us together to make safe and meaningful spaces in our communities where we and other species can be at home.

Learn more: Why Make Ecobricks?

How to make an Ecobrick?

Ecobricks are made by packing a PET bottle tight with plastics. First, save up and segregate your plastic! Keep it seperate from the organics-- if necessary clean it and dry it. First ecobrick? Start with a small bottle! Preferably, a bottle that you can easily get more of so eentually you can make a module. Get a solid stick (dowel, drumstick, branch, etc.) and start by inserting a a soft, colored plastic into the bottle's bottom. Stuff it down with other plastics of the same color until you have a 2-3cm base. Cut up other plastics and insert them losely into the bottle until it is almost full. Use your stick to pack the plastics down by pushing around on the sides. Fill, pack and repeat! Leave 1-2cm of space at the top of the bottle. Cap. Weigh your ecobrick. Divide weight by the bottle's volume: make sure you density is over the GEA's recomended minimum 0.33g/ml (a good ecobrick is at least 0.4g/ml). Log your ecobrick (keep a list or use our handy GoBrik.com app). Store securely. When you've got enough... Build!

Learn more: Comprehensive Ten Step Guide

What can you build with ecobricks?

So many things! Ecobricks can be connected using tire bands, silicone, cob, and cob to build furniture, gardens, structures and more. Ecobricks are being used in different ways around the world. From making a few sitting stools for a home to a cafe that use hundreds of lego modules to make their co-working space-- your imagination is the limit! The best way to build with ecobricks though is with earth. Ecobrick and earth building is ideal for making parks, gardens and green spaces of all kinds.

Learn more: General Ecobrick Applications

What makes ecobricks and ecobrick applications different from other plastic stuff?

The methods of making and building with Ecobricks that are endorsed by the Global Ecobrick Alliance are guided by regenerative principles. This means that they are net-zero, localized, mandalic, transcaste, earthen, circular and open source. Yep... that's alot of new words! And that's the point-- ecobricking is all about applying a whole different paradigm to our plastic, so that we avoid the mistakes, problems and pollution that come from the old way of doing things.

Learn more: Ecobrick Regenerative Principles

Why not just landfill or dump my plastic?

When plastic is dumped or landfilled it's full surface area ends up fully exposed to the sun, friction, fire and/or water. When this happens plastic degrades-- releasing CO2, other green house gases, and toxins. Eventually plastic breaks down into smaller and smaller pieces called micro-plastics. Increasingly, scientists are showing that these toxins and micro-plastic gets into ecosystems causing problems for plants and animals. Eventually, through the and eventually reach us and our children and cause all sorts of health problems for us. Because of this, its imporant to not let our plastic get loose into the biosphere by littering or dumping it.

Learn more: Plastic can be Poisonous

Why not just burn or send my plastic to be incienerated?

Whether plastic is burned on the side of the road or in a high-tech waste-to-energy plant, it is a tremendous waste. Plastic, as a fosil fuel by-product, is the result of hundreds of millions of years of work by the Earth to remove carbon from the atmosphere, compact and secure it and green the biosphere. As a dense form of carbon, when plastic burns, it's carbon is released loose back into the air. In many cases poisonous and toxic chemicals are also released. And it also leads to more plastic being generated. Because it is so easy to get rid of plastic by burning it, it makes it seem like there's no problem buying and using more. This in turn encourages more carbon deposits to be extracted, and more plastic made, thrown away and burned.

Learn more: Plastic can be Poisonous

Why not just recycle my plastic?

Recyling plastic is a great idea-- in theory! Unfortunately, when recycling is done enterprises that put profit-interest over the Earth-interests, it doesn't work so well. It is clearly shown that industrial recycling only cyles plastic a few times before it ends up loose in the biosphere. Industrial recycling also takes lots of energy. The transporation, processing and smelting of plastic is a process that is net-additive. In other words, it adds lots of carbon back into the biosphere. Recycling plastic, also leads to more products made from plastic. Making new plastic products not only requires lots of energy, it most often also requires new plastic to be mixed in. Putting plastics into industrial recycling puts energy into more industry, more plastic and more pollution.

Learn more: Industrial Plastic Recycling

So how's ecobricking any different from recycling, dumping or burning plastic?

Ecobricking is a form of plastic sequestration. That means there's six principled differences between ecobricking and industrial methods. Rather than being for-profit ecobricking is for-Earth. Rather than loosing plastic and carbon, ecobricking is all about compacting and securing them. Rather than downcycling plastic, ecobriking creates indefinitely reusable building blocks. Rather than rely on marketing that obscures ecological connection, ecobricking is a hands-on learning experience that raises awareness. Rather than result in more prodcuts and more factories that harm plants and animals, ecobricking leads to green spaces that give them a home. Rather than un-attentive disposal, ecobricking is all about measuring, tracking and disclosing our plastics! To sum it up... ecobricking is about following the Earth's example to make sure that we're transitioning from plastic to greener ways of living.

Learn more: Plastic Sequestration

Don't ecobricks just encourage people to consume more plastic?

This is a question we get a lot from people who have yet to make an ecobrick. Once you commit to taking full responsibility for your plastic and start packing every piece, you get very choosy about the plastic you buy! Let's be honest, ecobricking isn't fun-- nor washing, drying your plastics every day to prep them for bricking. In a 2021 cross-sectional survey of ecobrickers, we found that on average their monthly plastic consumption had decreased to 61% of their per capital average. Rather than give an excuse to continue consuming plastic, ecobricking helps to increase plastic awareness and accelerate plastic transition.

Learn more: Plastic Transition

I am keen to get going with ecobricking! What's the best way to start?

Start by writing down today's date. Then, save your plastic for the next month! Every single piece that comes into your household, be sure that it doesn't leave-- but instead gets cleaned, dried and placed in a special bin. After 30 days, weigh all the plastic that your household has consumed, transfer it to another bin and... begin ecobricking (see the how to ecobrick). If you don't finish all the plastic in one sitting that's ok. Keep putting the plastic you consume in bin #1 and every month transfer your ecobrickable plastic into bin #2. This way you're not only efficiently ecobricking, your tracking your consumption, plastic reduction and plastic transition.

Learn more: How to Make an Ecobrick

Where can I drop off my complete ecobricks?

Before contributing your ecobricks to a community project, it's essential to make sure your ecobricks are high quality-- no one wants bottles filled with loose dirty plastic! It's good to follow the GEA best practices. It's even better to get your ecobricks first authenticated by peers-- you can do that on our GoBrik app. When your ecobricks are ready, look for a community ecobrick hub near you. We've got a searchable map on our GoBrik app and a marketplace where you can search for ecobrick requests.

IMPORTANT: Remember that ecobricks are all about taking personal responsibility for our plastic-- not a fancy way to get rid of it! Putting your own ecobricks to use in your own projects is the essence of the movement. Of course, more and more there's bigger ecobrick building projects happening...

Learn more: More on Dropping Off Ecobricks

How can I get ecobricks for my project?

It's important to remember that ecobricks aren't 'bricks' in the tradition sense of the word. If you're keen to build something, in most cases your better off using normal bricks and construction techniques! But if you're keen to build a space for your community, you can set yourself up as an Ecobrick Hub-- where folks drop off their ecobricks to help you with your project.

Building Questions

What can I make with a few ecobricks?

Ecobrick modules are a simple, practical application of ecobricks. All you need are a dozen to start and you can build a simple stool that can later be combined with other modules to make tables and chairs. Milstein modules are the simplest type of module and allow you to build two layers vertically and expand horizontally to make couches, beds and even stages.

Learn more: More about Ecobrick Modules

What can I make with dozens of ecobricks?

Dieleman modules are an exciting way to make life-sized lego out of ecobricks. Dieleman modules are a geometric configuration of 16 ecobricks that enables vertical and horizontal inter-connection. These modules can be stacked horizontally and vertically indefinitely. Applications include indoor playgrounds, temporary stalls, sheds, and circular structure.

Learn more: More about Ecobrick Dieleman Lego

What can I make with thousands of ecobricks?

Ecobrick open spaces are interactive spaces created by mobilizing all of a community's ecobrick modules. Using dozens (or better hundreds!) of milstein and dieleman modules open spaces for sitting, talking, playing and more can be created for festivals, showcases, markets, fairs, etc. Open Spaces enable participants to playfully arrange modules to collaboratively create a space that best suits the needs of the moment.

What happens when a module comes to it's end?

Good quetion! It's important to think about the next life of things we make-- what we happen them when they come to their end. We provide guidelines to make sure all ecobrick applications-- like modules-- have their next life built in. By making modules with silicione or tire-bands, ecobricks can be removed from the module without damaging them. That means the module can be disbanded, rebuilty, repaired... or the ecobricks can be taken and used for an earth build. In other words, ecobrick applications, leverage the long-lasting properties of plastic and apply the principles of circular design, so that ecobricks can be used over and over again.

Learn more: More about Ecobrick Circular Design/h5>

What is Earth and Ecobrick building?

Building with earth is an ancient tradition. By combining it with ecobricks, anyone can build simple non-structural constructions. In particular, earth and ecobrick building is ideal for making planters, benches and garden beds-- parks, food-forests, and in general green spaces that can serve our community and be a home for plants and other species. Using local earth to make cob mortar, our ecobricks are covered up and secured from all forms of degradation.

Learn more: Earth & Ecobrick Buidling/h5>

How to build with Ecobricks and Earth?

Building with earth and ecobricks is... well, pretty awesome really. First you start by learning about the ancient traditions in your area. Then you get your ecobricks, friends and community together. Using nothing but local materials you then get down and dirty digging a foundation, laying it down and building on up. Layer by layer you add ecobricks, cob, ecobricks, cob. As you go you curve and sculpt your bench, planter or wall. When it's up and it's dry then you choose the appropriate outer skin and render it on.
That's obviously too short a summary! If you're serious, you'll want to check out our step by step directions and suggested techniques.

Learn more: Earth & Ecobrick Buidling/h5>

Are ecobricks a fire hazard since they are made with flammable plastic?

Plastic, just like pretty much everything else, can be flamable. Whether your building with ecobricks or wood, it's all about building safely. It all starts by making ecobricks properly. When ecobricks are made according to GEA density standards, flammability is greatly reduced. Furthermore, by building and using ecobricks properly, their fire hazard is further reduced. Ecobrick and earth building for green spaces, our most recommended ecobrick application, reduces flammability to virtually nil.

Learn more: Ecobricks & Fire Security

More Questions

What about plastic I pick up in on the beach-- can it be ecobricked?

Yes... and no! Normal ecobricks are all about the mindful repurposing of the plastic that we consume personally. This means caring for our plastic by segregating, cleaning and drying it. The plastic that has washed up on the beach (or we find on the road side) tends to be wet, dirty and chunky. So its not ideal for putting into a normal ecobrick. However, it poses a contamination danger to the environment just as much as any other plastic. To take care of it, you can pack it into what we call an ocean ecobrick. Ocean ecobricks are made by cutting two PET bottles, packing both tight, and merging them into one.

Learn more: Ocean Ecobricks

What about cigarette filters? Can they be ecobricked?

YES. Cigarette filters are made from plastic, and although small, are one of the biggest source of plastic pollution. We've thus come up with a special technique and ecobricking just for cigarette butts. Whenyou're done smoking, simply peel of the paper and remove the ash (this can be safely discarded). Then, place the filter into your ecobrick. If you're really keen, you can make an ecobrick entirely out of cigarette filters. We call this a Cigbrick!

Learn more: Cigbricks

GoBrik Questions

What is this GoBrik app for ecobricking?

The GoBrik platform serves ecobrickers around the world, enabling them to log, manage, validate, authenticate and exchange their ecobricks. Ecobricks that are logged on GoBrik are given a serial number and their unique data (weight, color, volume, density, maker etc.) is saved and managed in the database. Once logged, ecobricks are automatically submitted to the validation queue where they are reviewed by the community of ecobrickers. Ecobricks that are successfully authenticated (the reviewers concur on the visible data) are then eligible to be exchanged and traded on the platform.

Learn more About GoBrik

 

What are these Brikcoins I keep hearing about?

Brikcoins (ß) are a new medium of exchange founded on community collaboration and a healthy planet. Brikcoins are based on ecobricked, sequestered, plastic. Brikcoins are earned by helping to authenticate ecobricks on GoBrik and prove the sequestration of plastic. Every Brikcoin is backed by 100g of packed and secured plastic. Anyone with an account can start earning earning Brikcoins which can then be used to pay for GEA course, exchange with GoBrik users, and more.

Learn more about Brikcoins
